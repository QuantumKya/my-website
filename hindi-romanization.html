Pronouncing Hindi vowels!

Vowels / स्वर

Vowels in Hindi (other than "a") are attached to consonants to make consonant-vowel pairs, e.g. "ka / क" to "kā / का"

For demonstrative purposes, the consonant क​ "k" will be used for future vowel examples.

a / अ

-pronounced "uh" /ə/, as in "sun"

on consonants, this is the "default" vowel, with the character appearing the same; e.g. क

ā / आ

-pronounced "ah" /aː/, as in "blot"

appears on consonants as a straight line to the right of the character, e.g. का

ē / ए

-pronounced "eh (canadian)" /e/, as in "maybe"

appears on consonants as a lightly curved lines protruding from the top of the character, e.g. के

ai / ऐ

-pronounced "ehh" /ɛː/, as in "fair"

appears on consonants as two lightly curved lines on top, e.g. कै

i / इ

-pronounced "ih" /ɪ/, as in "wick"

appears on consonants as a line to the left connected to the character with a curve above, e.g. कि

ī / ई

-pronounced "ee" /iː/, as in "leech"

appears on consonants also as a connected line, but to the right of the character, e.g. की

ō / ओ

-pronounced "oh" /oː/, as in "orange"

appears on consonants as a line to the right of the character with some extra decoration, e.g. को

au

-pronounced "aw" /ɔː/, as in "awesome"

appears on consonants similarly to "o", but with two lines decorating the vowel, e.g. कौ

u

-pronounced "euh" /ʊ/, as in "put"

appears on consonants as a small curve underneath the character that points up as if it could hold water, e.g. कु

ū

-pronounced "oo" /uː/, as in "room"

appears on consonants as a similar under-curve, but points down and thus is NOT a good container, e.g. कू

There are technically more vowels, but they're not important, and their status as vowels is debated anyway. I won't be surprised if they go the way of Pluto.
